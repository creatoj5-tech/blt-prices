Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted but devices cannot be stolen — verified using checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty, no Model “N” number. Activated Apple SWAP = Grade A price.

SWAP: Genuine Apple swaps only, “N” model number, never activated. Activated Apple SWAP = Grade A price.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones that have been remanufactured or refurbished and are marked on the back. Take $50 lower than Grade B pricing.

NEW Sealed: Brand new, sealed in box, warranty not activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand new, open box, film on phone, all accessories sealed, warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand new, sealed in box, warranty has already been activated.

Warranty: Newest models need to be covered under Apple's 1-year warranty. Replaced / red IMEI / expired devices may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like new / mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked glass / good LCD, 1 small white spot. Multiple or major spots = bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues. Must be a fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket screen also = bad LCD. Black LCD / unable to test = DOA.

DOA: No power / signs of life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, mixed issues. Heavy bend / damage / missing parts may be subject to lower price.

Service / Degraded Battery %: Batteries that show “Service” or are degraded (generally under 80%) are subject to deduction.

Genuine / Used Repair Messages: Genuine or used repair messages (display, battery, camera, etc.) are subject to deduction.

Unknown Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: Common issue — keyboard or app images burned into LCD (check on white screen). Mostly seen on Samsung and iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: Common issue — Face ID must be fully working. Test Face ID and confirm it reads / registers your face.

Camera Spotting: Common issue, especially iPhone 12 / 13 series. Check zoomed in (2.5x – 3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint True Tone: Missing True Tone = high likelihood of aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen = bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: Used = cracked price; if cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera at different zooms; cracked lens often comes with heavy spotting.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: No time limit. If a phone relocks after sale, or a Samsung becomes Trade In / KG Lock down the line, the seller is responsible — BLT will offer a deduction or return.
