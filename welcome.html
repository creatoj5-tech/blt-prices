Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted but devices cannot be stolen — verified via checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty, no Model 'N' Number. ACTIVATED APPLE SWAP = GRADE A PRICE.

SWAP: Genuine Apple Swaps Only — 'N' Model Number, NEVER ACTIVATED. ACTIVATED APPLE SWAP = GRADE A PRICE.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones marked as Remanufactured / Refurbished on the back. Priced $50 lower than Grade B pricing.

NEW Sealed: Brand NEW / Sealed in Box, warranty not activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand NEW / Open Box — film on phone, all accessories sealed, warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand NEW / Sealed in Box, warranty HAS BEEN activated.

Warranty: Newest models need to be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. If not (Replaced, Red IMEI, expired, etc.) may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like New / Mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked Glass / Good LCD, 1 small white spot allowed; multiple/major spots = Bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues — must be a fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket also = Bad LCD. BLACK LCD / UNABLE TO TEST = DOA.

DOA: No Power / No Signs of Life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, or mixed issues. Heavy bend / damage / missing parts may be subject to lower price.

Service / Degraded Battery %: Batteries showing 'Service' or degraded (generally under 80% battery) — subject to deduction.

Genuine / Used Repair Messages: Display, Battery, Camera, etc. — subject to deduction. BLT does its best but varies by device.

Unknown Repair Messages: Feel free to ASK, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: COMMON ISSUE — images (keyboard / apps) burnt into LCD. Check on white screen. Mostly seen on Samsung / iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: COMMON ISSUE — Face ID must be fully working. Test that it reads / registers your face (not just shows it).

Camera Spotting: COMMON ISSUE on iPhone 12 / 13 series (other series can have it too). Check zoomed in (2.5x – 3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint — True Tone: Check phones for True Tone. Missing True Tone = high likelihood of aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket Screen = Bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: If Used = Cracked price; if Cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera at different zooms — cracked lens often has heavy spotting.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: NO TIME LIMIT — if phone relocks after sale, or Samsung becomes Trade In / KG Lock down the line, seller is responsible for deduction or return.
