Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted, but devices cannot be stolen. We verify with checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days of warranty start. No model "N" number. Apple Swap that has been activated = Grade A price (NOT HSO).

SWAP: Genuine Apple Swap only. Model number must start with "N" and the device must NEVER have been activated. Activated Apple Swap = Grade A price.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones marked on the back as Remanufactured / Refurbished. Priced $50 below Grade B.

NEW Sealed: Brand new, sealed in box, warranty NOT activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand new, open box, film still on phone, accessories sealed, warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand new, sealed in box, warranty HAS been activated.

Warranty: Newest models must be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. Replaced / red IMEI / expired devices may receive an additional deduction.

Grade A: Like New / Mint condition. This is the default ceiling for any USED phone unless the seller explicitly says brand new + no box (then HSO/SWAP applies).

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked glass with good LCD, OR 1 small white spot. Multiple / major LCD spots = bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues. Must be a fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket screens = bad LCD. Black LCD or unable to test = DOA.

DOA: No power / no signs of life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, mixed issues. Heavy bend / damage / missing parts may receive a lower price.

Service Battery / Degraded Battery: Batteries showing "Service" or under ~80% health are subject to deduction.

Genuine / Used Repair Messages: Display, Battery, Camera repair messages are subject to deduction. We do our best but the deduction varies by device.

Unknown Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but devices will be deducted.

LCD Burns: Common on Samsung and iPhone X and newer. Check on a white screen for keyboard/app images burned in. Subject to deduction.

Bad Face ID: Face ID must fully read and register a face. A device that shows a face but does not register = bad Face ID. Subject to deduction.

Camera Spotting: Common on iPhone 12 and 13 series. Check zoomed in (2.5x–3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint — True Tone: Missing True Tone usually indicates an aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen = bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: Used = cracked price. If already cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Often comes with heavy camera spotting.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: NO TIME LIMIT. If a phone relocks after sale, or a Samsung becomes Trade-In / KG Lock, the seller is responsible for a deduction or return.
