Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted, but devices cannot be stolen (verified via checkmend.com).

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty. No Model 'N' Number. Activated Apple SWAP = Grade A price.

SWAP: Genuine Apple Swaps only, 'N' model number, never activated. Activated Apple SWAP = Grade A price.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones marked 'Remanufactured' or 'Refurbished' on the back. Priced $50 lower than Grade B.

NEW Sealed: Brand new, sealed in box. Warranty not yet activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand new, open box. Film on phone, all accessories sealed, warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand new, sealed in box. Warranty has been activated.

Warranty: Newest models must be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. If 'Replaced', red IMEI, or expired, may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like new / mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked glass / good LCD, 1 small white spot. Multiple/major spots = bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues, must be fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket also = bad LCD. Black LCD / unable to test = DOA.

DOA: No power / signs of life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, mixed issues. Heavy bend / damage / missing parts may be subject to lower price.

Service / Degraded Battery: Batteries that show 'Service' or are degraded (generally under 80%) — subject to deduction.

Genuine / Used Repair Messages: Genuine or Used repair messages (Display, Battery, Camera, etc.) are subject to deduction.

Unknown Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: Common issue — images (keyboard, apps) burned into LCD. Check on white screen. Mostly seen on Samsung and iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: Face ID must be fully working — test that it reads and registers your face, not just shows it.

Camera Spotting: Common issue on iPhone 12 / 13 series. Check zoomed in (2.5x – 3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint — True Tone: Check phones for True Tone. Missing True Tone = high likelihood of aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen = bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: Used = cracked price. If already cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera at multiple zoom levels — cracked lens often comes with heavy spotting.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: No time limit. If a phone relocks after sale, or a Samsung becomes Trade-In / KG Lock down the line, the seller is responsible for deduction or return.
