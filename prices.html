Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted but devices cannot be stolen, verified using checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty. No Model "N" Number. Activated Apple SWAP is priced as Grade A.

SWAP: Genuine Apple Swaps only. Must have "N" Model Number. Never activated. Activated Apple SWAP is priced as Grade A.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones that have been "Remanufactured" or "Refurbished" and are marked on the back. Priced $50 lower than Grade B pricing.

NEW Sealed: Brand new, sealed in box, warranty NOT yet activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand new, open box. Film is on phone, all accessories sealed, warranty must be within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand new, sealed in box, but warranty HAS BEEN activated.

Warranty: Newest models need to be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. Replaced, Red IMEI, expired warranty etc. may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like new / mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked Glass with good LCD. One small white spot is acceptable. Multiple or major spots count as Bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD or touch issues. Must be a fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket screens count as Bad LCD. Black LCD or unable to test counts as DOA.

DOA: No power or signs of life. Cracked screens, Bad LCD, water damage, mixed issues. Heavy bend, damage, or missing parts may be subject to lower price.

"Service" or Degraded Battery %: Batteries that show "Service" or are degraded (generally under 80%) are subject to deduction.

"Genuine / Used" Repair Messages: Subject to deduction (Display, Battery, Camera). BLT does its best but result varies depending on device.

"Unknown" Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: Common issue. Images such as keyboard or apps burnt into LCD. Check on white screen. Mostly seen on Samsung and iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: Common issue. Face ID must be fully working - test that it reads and registers your face, not just shows it without reading.

Camera Spotting: Common issue for iPhone 12 and 13 series (other series can also have this). Check zoomed in (2.5x to 3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint - True Tone: Check phones for True Tone. Missing True Tone = high likelihood of aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen counts as Bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: Used = cracked price. If cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera at different zooms - cracked lens often comes with heavy spotting.

Bad Back Camera: Subject to deduction. Ask for current rate.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: No time limit. If a phone re-locks after sale, or a Samsung becomes Trade-In / KG Lock down the line, the seller is responsible for the deduction or can return the device.

Battery Cycle Count: If over 5 cycle count, NEW battery will be priced as USED.
