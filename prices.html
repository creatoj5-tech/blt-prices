Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted but devices cannot be stolen -- utilizing checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty, no Model "N" number. ACTIVATED APPLE SWAP = GRADE A PRICE.

SWAP: Genuine Apple Swaps Only. "N" Model Number / NEVER ACTIVATED. ACTIVATED APPLE SWAP = GRADE A PRICE.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones that have been "Remanufactured" / "Refurbished" and are marked on the back. Can take $50 lower than "Grade B" pricing.

NEW / Sealed: Brand NEW / Sealed in Box. Warranty not activated.

NEW / Open: Brand NEW / Open Box. Film on phone, all accessories sealed, warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand NEW / Sealed in Box. Warranty HAS BEEN activated.

Warranty: Newest models need to be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. If not "Replaced" / Red IMEI, expired, etc., may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like New / Mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked glass / good LCD, 1 small white spot. Multiple / major spots = Bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues. Must be fully functional phone, just needs new screen. Aftermarket screen also = Bad LCD. BLACK LCD / UNABLE TO TEST = DOA.

DOA: No power / signs of life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, mixed issues. Too heavy bend / damage / missing parts may be subject to lower price.

"Service" / Degraded Battery: Batteries that show "Service" or are degraded (generally under 80% battery) -- subject to deduction.

"Genuine / Used" Repair Messages: "Genuine" / "Used" repair messages are subject to deduction (Display / Battery / Camera, etc.). Will do our best but varies depending on device.

"Unknown" Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: COMMON ISSUE TO LOOK OUT FOR -- Images (keyboard / apps burnt into LCD; check on white screen). Mostly seen on Samsung / iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: COMMON ISSUE TO LOOK OUT FOR -- Face ID MUST be fully working. Test Face ID and make sure it reads / registers your face (not just shows it, but actually reads).

Camera Spotting: COMMON ISSUE TO LOOK OUT FOR -- Common issue for iPhone 12 / 13 series (other series can have it too). Check zoomed in (2.5x - 3x zoom). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint: Check phones for True Tone. Missing True Tone = high likelihood of aftermarket screen (not 100% of the time, but most of the time screen will be aftermarket).

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen = Bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: Used = Cracked price. If Cracked = OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera (including different zooms) -- often cracked lens can have heavy spotting also. Subject to additional deduction.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: NO TIME LIMIT -- if phone were to relock after sale, or Samsung becomes Trade-In / KG Lock down the line, seller is responsible for deduction or can return.
