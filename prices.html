Device Grading Guide

ESN: Bad ESN is accepted, but devices must not be stolen — verified via checkmend.com.

HSO: Within 30 days into warranty, no model 'N' number. Activated Apple SWAP equals Grade A price.

SWAP: Genuine Apple SWAPs only, 'N' model number, never activated. Activated Apple SWAP equals Grade A price.

Asurion / Valutech: Phones that have been remanufactured or refurbished and marked on the back. Take $50 lower than Grade B pricing.

NEW Sealed: Brand new, sealed in box; warranty not activated.

NEW Open Box: Brand new, Open Box; film on phone, all accessories sealed; warranty within 30 days of activation.

Sealed (Activated): Brand new, sealed in box; warranty has been activated.

Warranty: Newest models must be covered under Apple 1-year warranty. If not — Replaced, red IMEI, expired, etc. — may be eligible for additional deduction.

Grade A: Like new, mint condition.

Grade B: OEM, no cracks, light use, fully functional.

Grade C: Cracked glass with good LCD; one small white spot. Multiple or major spots equal bad LCD. Must be OEM.

Grade D: Bad LCD, touch issues, must be a fully functional phone that just needs a new screen. Aftermarket also equals bad LCD. Black LCD or unable to test equals DOA.

DOA: No power or signs of life; cracked, bad LCD, water damaged, mixed issues. Heavy bend, damage, or missing parts may be subject to lower price.

Service / Degraded Battery: Batteries that show 'Service' or are degraded (generally under 80% battery) are subject to deduction.

Genuine / Used Repair Messages: Genuine or Used repair messages are subject to deduction (display, battery, camera, etc.). BLT does its best but it varies depending on device.

Unknown Repair Messages: Feel free to ask, but items will be deducted.

LCD Burns: Common issue — images such as keyboard or apps burnt into LCD. Check on a white screen. Mostly seen on Samsung and iPhone X and newer.

Bad Face ID: Common issue — Face ID must be fully working. Test Face ID and verify it reads and registers your face, not just shows it.

Camera Spotting: Common issue, especially iPhone 12 / iPhone 13 series. Check zoomed in (2.5x to 3x). Subject to additional deduction.

Helpful Hint — True Tone: Check phones for True Tone. Missing True Tone is a high-likelihood indicator of an aftermarket screen.

Heavy Scratching: Subject to additional deduction.

Heavy Edge Wear: Subject to additional deduction.

Aftermarket (AM) Screen: Aftermarket screen equals bad LCD.

Small (1x) LCD Spot: If used and uncracked, priced as cracked. If already cracked, OK.

Cracked Lens: Subject to additional deduction. Check camera at different zooms; cracked lens often comes with heavy spotting.

Unlocked Relocks / KG Lock: No time limit. If a phone relocks after sale, or a Samsung becomes Trade-In or KG Locked later, the seller is responsible for the deduction or can return.
